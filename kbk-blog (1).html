For a second,
I became a theist.

By Anonymous

I walked into a temple a skeptic. I walked out unsettled, not convinced, not converted, just carrying questions I couldn't shake.

Does God exist? And if he does, why does any of this look the way it does?

Why are some people born into lives so consumed by survival that they never get a moment to even ask why they're alive? Why does a tradition that preaches oneness spend so much energy deciding who belongs and who doesn't?

Nobody asked us if we wanted consciousness. Nobody asked us if we were ready to feel. We were just handed awareness, the ability to question, to suffer, to wonder, without a single say in the matter.

Like a hen bred by science, born into existence without consent, living a life it never chose, for a purpose it will never understand. Maybe we aren't so different. Maybe we were made the same way. Out of curiosity. Out of experiment. Without being asked.

Maybe we were never part of a plan.
Maybe we were an accident.

Something that spilled out of divine curiosity and couldn't be taken back. God didn't create out of love. He created out of wonder. And then couldn't undo what wonder had started.

So here we are. Caught between a God who can't abandon us and can't fully care for us. Not a parent watching a child make mistakes, but a parent who never fully wanted the child, yet can't bring themselves to let go.

And we, we took that free will and built systems that told certain people they don't belong. That some lives matter less. That some people were born on the wrong side of something they never chose.

Those lives were real. They had one chance at this. And they spent it being invisible.

And yet, millions of us still show up. Still offer everything we have to something that may never fully answer back.

The unwanted child still loves the parent.

Maybe that's the most human thing there is, loving without guarantee, giving without certainty of return.

· · ·

I walked into a temple an atheist. I walked out agnostic. Something shifted in a moment I can't fully explain.

Maybe that's enough for now.

Anonymous

Written somewhere between belief and doubt
